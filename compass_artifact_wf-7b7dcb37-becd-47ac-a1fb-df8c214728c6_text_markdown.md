# Rebutting the Claim That "Epoxy Is Hard to Obtain and Difficult to Use in FABs in India"

## TL;DR
- The contractor's claim is **factually unsupported**. Epoxy flooring/coatings are an industry-standard, often *specified* material for semiconductor fabs, display factories, and electronics cleanrooms worldwide — chosen for seamlessness, chemical resistance, ESD control, and particle/AMC control.
- Every major global epoxy and resin-flooring supplier (Sika, BASF/Master Builders Solutions, Fosroc, Pidilite, Asian Paints, Flowcrete, Kukdo, Aditya Birla, Atul) has a manufacturing and/or distribution presence in India, with cleanroom-grade products (e.g., Sikafloor-269 CR / 269 ECF CR) catalogued and stocked locally.
- Cleanroom-certified epoxy systems already exist and are sold in India to ISO 14644-1 Class 4/5 and GMP Class A standards, with ESD variants meeting ANSI/ESD S20.20 and IEC 61340-5-1 — directly contradicting "hard to obtain / difficult to use."

---

## Key Findings
1. Epoxy is used across multiple FAB zones — clean production zones, sub-fab, ESD-protected areas, machine/electrical rooms, and under raised access floors — and is recommended by industry-standard references for these environments.
2. The world's top epoxy companies almost all operate in India (subsidiaries, JVs, or distributors), and India is itself one of the largest epoxy resin markets and producers (Grasim/Aditya Birla is the #1 liquid epoxy resin producer in India).
3. India has a deep local supply chain: global subsidiaries (Sika India, BASF/Master Builders India, Fosroc India) plus domestic majors (Pidilite, Asian Paints, Aditya Birla, Atul) with stock, distributor networks, and India-tested products.
4. Cleanroom/ESD epoxy is sold and installed in India with documented ISO 14644 and ANSI/ESD certifications.

## STEP 1 — Epoxy Usage in FABs

**KEY FINDINGS:** Epoxy flooring and coatings are a standard, widely used material in semiconductor fabs, display plants, and electronics cleanrooms. They are applied in clean production zones, sub-fab areas, ESD-protected areas, machine/electrical rooms, and to coat structural concrete beneath raised access floors. Selection is driven by seamlessness, chemical resistance, ESD control, ease of cleaning, and low particle/AMC emissions.

**EVIDENCE DATA:**
- *Semiconductor Digest* ("Keeping on Track with Cleanroom Floors, Walls and Ceilings"): "Epoxy or other protective coatings are often used to coat the structural concrete beneath the raised floors of semiconductor fabs." Epoxy enamel is also used to finish cleanroom ceiling metal panels.
- Sherwin-Williams M&P Facilities ("Designing Semiconductor Cleanroom Flooring Systems for Modern Fab Clean Zones"): semiconductor/microelectronics often demand ISO 3 where wafers are exposed and ISO 4–5 elsewhere; flooring must control Airborne Molecular Contamination (AMC) and outgassing.
- Allied Cleanrooms (Waferfab Cleanrooms): customization options include "FR-rated wall panels, epoxy flooring, and ESD-protected surfaces" for ISO 4–8 fabs.
- Cleanroomflooring.com: "Self-leveling epoxy systems create a seamless, non-porous surface ideal for ISO 5-8 cleanrooms. Chemical-resistant, easy to decontaminate, and available with anti-static (ESD) properties."
- Reasons epoxy is chosen: seamless/monolithic (no joints for contaminants), chemical resistance (IPA, hydrogen peroxide, acids, alkalis), ESD/static control, easy cleaning/decontamination, durability under trolley/forklift traffic, low VOC/AMC emissions.
- Standards referenced: ISO 14644-1 (cleanroom particle classification), ISO 14644-8 (AMC), ANSI/ESD S20.20, IEC 61340-5-1 (ESD), EN 13813 (resin screeds), BS 8204-6 (synthetic resin flooring), FeRFA guidance.
- Product types: ESD/conductive epoxy (carbon-loaded), static-dissipative epoxy, solvent-free/total-solid epoxy, self-leveling/self-smoothing epoxy, epoxy-cement hybrids, novolac (chemical-resistant) epoxy.

**REBUTTAL USAGE POINT:** Epoxy is not merely "usable" in fabs — it is an industry-default, standards-referenced flooring/coating material for fab clean and support zones. The claim that epoxy is "difficult to use in FABs" contradicts published industry practice and product certifications.

## STEP 5 — Cleanroom Epoxy Usage Cases (by ISO classification)

**KEY FINDINGS:** Epoxy is one of the three flooring families (with vinyl and rubber) accepted for cleanroom environments, and is specifically certified to ISO 14644-1 cleanroom classes and ESD standards. Cleanroom-grade epoxy products are sold in India.

**EVIDENCE DATA:**
- **Sikafloor-269 CR** (sold in India): "two part, total solid, low particle and ultra-low VOC/AMC emission, self-smoothing epoxy resin system designed for cleanroom environments." Certified: ISO 14644-1 **Class 5** (CSM Report SI 0908-494) and GMP **Class A** (SI1008-533); ISO 14644-8 outgassing class -9.6; biological resistance per ISO 846; fire per EN 13501-1.
- **Sikafloor-269 ECF CR** (sold in India): electrostatic-conductive cleanroom epoxy; certified ISO 14644-1 **Class 4**; meets ATEX 137. Used as wearing course of Sikafloor MultiDur ES-28 ECF/EQ system.
- Sika cleanroom CSM (Cleanroom Suitable Materials) qualifications per ISO 14644 / GMP, listed in the Fraunhofer IPA database (tested-device.com); a documented Fraunhofer reference cleanroom used Sikafloor-269 CR ECF on the lower concrete floor.
- **Fosroc Nitoflor SL2000** (India): "dense, impervious, coloured and chemically resistant floor surface which is hygienic and easy to clean… Typical applications include: Clean rooms. Laboratories."; Nitoflor SL Conductive/Dissipative for electronics ESD areas.
- **BASF MasterTop BC 372EPA:** fiber-free electrostatically conductive ESD component for EPAs (IT components, photovoltaics, EV batteries, labs).
- ESD epoxy in cleanrooms maintains surface resistance 10⁶–10⁹ Ω, meeting IEC 61340-5-1 and ANSI/ESD S20.20. Stonhard's Stonlux ESD data sheet states it "has been specifically designed to comply with the ANSI/ESD S20.20 specification," with point-to-point/point-to-ground readings required to "fall below 1.0x10⁶ ohms (Ω)" (stonhard.ca product data).
- Cleanroom Floors Inc. case: 15,000 sq ft semiconductor-component facility with heavy-duty epoxy finishing in areas specified to ISO 5 (Class 100) to ISO 7 (Class 10,000).
- **India installations:** Asian Paints ESD coatings tested by CPRI; numerous Indian applicators offer ESD/cleanroom epoxy for electronics/pharma cleanrooms.

**REBUTTAL USAGE POINT:** Cleanroom epoxy is not experimental or scarce — it is a certified, catalogued category meeting the exact ISO 14644 and ESD standards fabs require, and the leading cleanroom-grade products are explicitly sold in India.

## COMPREHENSIVE REBUTTAL SUMMARY

1. **Epoxy is an industry-standard fab material, not a difficult one.** Published industry sources (Semiconductor Digest, Sherwin-Williams M&P, Allied Cleanrooms) document epoxy coating of structural concrete under raised floors, epoxy cleanroom floors for ISO 5–8 zones, and ESD epoxy for electrostatic-protected areas — refuting "difficult to use in FABs."

2. **Cleanroom-grade epoxy is certified to the exact standards fabs require and is sold in India.** Sikafloor-269 CR (ISO 14644-1 Class 5 / GMP Class A) and Sikafloor-269 ECF CR (ISO 14644-1 Class 4, ESD, ATEX) are catalogued on Sika India's website with India-based test certifications (ERDA Vadodara, CFTRI Mysore).

3. **Every major global epoxy supplier operates in India.** Sika (Sika India, Navi Mumbai; CHF 11.2 billion 2025 sales, subsidiaries in 103 countries), BASF/Master Builders Solutions India, Fosroc India (Bangalore), Kukdo (India subsidiary), plus domestic majors Aditya Birla/Grasim (the #1 liquid epoxy resin producer in India, 246,000 t/yr after its Vilayat expansion), Atul, Pidilite, and Asian Paints — refuting "hard to obtain."

4. **Real electronics/semiconductor facilities use epoxy/ESD resin flooring.** Stonhard's Plexus project (over 225,000 sq ft total, including ~54,600 sq ft of conductive Stonlux ESD) and Flowcrete's Polyplas project are named, manufacturer-published electronics-manufacturing cases; brand-official Stonhard and Sherwin-Williams materials confirm epoxy systems engineered specifically for semiconductor fabs.

5. **India's own fabs are being built now with cleanroom scopes epoxy suppliers can serve.** Per Tata Group, the Tata–PSMC Dholera fab carries up to ₹91,000 crore investment and up to 50,000 wafers/month capacity (construction ~50% complete, trial production targeted late 2026 per Dholera Times); Micron Sanand has a 500,000 sq ft cleanroom — both in active cleanroom build-out, with all major epoxy suppliers maintaining Indian operations, distributor networks, and stock to serve them.

---

## Caveats
- Flagship fabs (Samsung, TSMC, Micron, Intel) generally do **not** publicly name their flooring/coating vendors; fab flooring specs are confidential. The Intel/Chandler case rests on a contractor's self-published marketing page and is **[Needs Verification]**.
- No flooring-vendor case study has yet been published for the specific new Indian fabs (Tata Dholera, Micron Sanand), as these are still under construction.
- Some market-size and growth figures in cited reports are projections; they are used here only to establish the scale and presence of supply, not as guarantees.
- A minority of technical sources note epoxy's downsides (charge generation on non-ESD epoxy; repair/contamination challenges in operational cleanrooms), which is why ESD/conductive epoxy variants and proper specification are used — this is a design consideration, not evidence of unavailability or unusability.
